Beef Shin Lasagna

Description

This beef shin lasagna is a rich and deeply flavorful twist on the classic Italian dish. Slow-cooked beef shin is braised until it’s fall-apart tender, then shredded and layered with a deeply savory tomato and red wine sauce. The long cooking time allows the collagen in the beef to melt into the sauce, giving it a luxurious, velvety texture that sets it apart from traditional ground beef versions.

Each layer is built with silky béchamel, sheets of pasta, and generous spoonfuls of the slow-cooked beef ragù. After baking, the top becomes golden and bubbling, with crisped edges and gooey melted cheese. It’s the kind of comforting, hearty meal that’s perfect for a slow Sunday or a special occasion — one bite and you’ll know it was worth the wait.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs beef shin, cut into large chunks
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 carrots, chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 cup red wine
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 12 lasagna noodles
  • 2 cups béchamel sauce (homemade or store-bought)
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Fresh basil for garnish

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large pot, heat some oil over medium heat. Add the beef shin and brown on all sides. Remove and set aside.
  3. Add the onion, carrots, and celery to the pot. Sauté until softened.
  4. Add the garlic and cook for another minute.
  5. Stir in the crushed tomatoes, red wine, beef broth, tomato paste, oregano, basil, salt, and pepper.
  6. Return the beef to the pot. Bring to a simmer, then cover and cook on low heat for 2-3 hours, or until the beef is tender.
  7. Remove the beef, shred it with two forks, and return it to the sauce.
  8. Cook the lasagna noodles according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  9. In a baking dish, spread a layer of the beef sauce, followed by a layer of noodles, béchamel sauce, and mozzarella cheese. Repeat the layers until all ingredients are used, finishing with béchamel and mozzarella on top.
  10. Sprinkle with Parmesan cheese.
  11. Cover with foil and bake for 30 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 15-20 minutes, or until golden and bubbling.
  12. Let it rest for 10-15 minutes before serving. Garnish with fresh basil.
  13. Serve hot and enjoy your delicious beef shin lasagna!
  14. Pair with a side salad and garlic bread for a complete meal.
